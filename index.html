Vision

As we may have noticed quite often, a professional photographer takes photos different from a normal person. What are the professional efforts to make a better shot if using the same equipment?

As one of the architects who design buildings, I rarely know the way people perceive buildings, such as locations where they tend to stand and frame their views, features which draw their attention and even details that they care about. Through computing a series of photos from social media such as Instagram and reconstructing a set of 3d points including viewpoints, We can now visualize those information, hidden or not so obvious otherwise, and compare those with the ones that framed by the photographers or the architects. As a result, on the one hand, the photograph techniques can be represented to the general public, on the other, urban photos in the social media at large, which are shaped by the space, can be analysed and thus inform designers.

So here is the hypothesis: Computer Vision can improve the way people navigate the urban space and inform how we design it, virtually then physically.

Overview

In the past decade, we have been able to reconstruct 3d model of a given scene or a building through computation based on a series of overlapping images. There are existing application to use these reconstructed 3d information for urban tourism (Snavely, 2006). However, the attempts have been focusing more on the 3D representation, in which the relation between the viewpoints and the subject is less investigated. This project is to map out the location of the viewpoints on a site plan in 2D. The users can thus navigate the Instagram photos by referencing their individual viewpoint which can be filtered by brushing the time bar. A photo of the building facade is also shown adjacently to highlight the computer detectable features such as contour and texture, which is closely related to human perception.

More about the research: Collective Visual Field is to examine architecture based on internet photos as the collective visual perception. The workflow of the project: Processing and generating 3D points cloud from internet photos and its data-set, Visualizing the data-set. A photo represents the visual consumption of a specific subject in a particular moment, which contains information of a localized viewpoint and the interest of the viewer. This project is to examine the visual perception in a collective way in the built environment through internet photos. As those photos are created with time and site specifically, we are able to investigate a pattern about the viewerâ€™s behavior in the context of built environment through the geoData of the area, the exact time of the photo that was taken, the camera coordinates and the 3D point coordinates of the subject. SFM (structure from motion) is implemented to compute the dataSet.
